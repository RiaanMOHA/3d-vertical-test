## Problems encountered and how they were solved

### Problem 1: Storage was double the listed area
- Symptom: Build had storage at 1.62 m² (1.80 × 0.90), blueprint says 0.81 m².
- Cause: I rounded room dimensions to a clean grid in the previous session without checking the listed areas.
- Fix: Shrunk to `x=5.40..W, z=0.90..1.80` (0.90 × 0.90 = 0.81 m²) and added L-shaped corridor extension to fill the freed space.

### Problem 2: Toilet had no door at all
- Symptom: Listed all gaps in the toilet's walls — there was no opening anywhere.
- Cause: Original wall code line `wallX(group, 0.90, 4.50, W)` had no `gaps=[]` parameter, so the toilet's south wall was solid.
- Fix: Added door gap. Then later moved it to the toilet's west wall (corridor side) for natural Japanese-house flow.

### Problem 3: Pole-like wall stubs (0.15 m walls rendering as columns)
- Symptom: User screenshot showed isolated vertical columns inside rooms.
- Cause: Door gaps positioned with 0.15 m of wall remaining on one side. A 0.15 × 0.10 × 2.40 m box looks like a column, not a wall fragment.
- Fix: Adjusted door gap ranges to be flush against the corner (zero stub) where possible, or keep stubs ≥ 0.20 m. Examples:
  - Toilet door changed from `[[4.65, 5.35]]` to `[[4.50, 5.20]]` — flush west.
  - Storage door changed from `[[1.95, 2.55]]` to `[[1.80, 2.50]]` — flush west.
  - Stair door changed from `[[0.10, 0.75]]` to `[[0, 0.65]]` — flush west.
- Lesson: future builds should use a helper or check that any wall segment of length < 0.20 m is collapsed.

### Problem 4: Orphan wall section
- Symptom: A vertical wall at `x=4.50, z=4.20..4.50` standing in the middle of the LDK with no rooms on either side.
- Cause: I had built a single `wallZ(group, 4.50, 4.20, 5.40)` covering both the LDK-strip area (z=4.20..4.50, both sides LDK) and the toilet west wall (z=4.50..5.40, valid). Only the southern half was real.

### Wall stubs ≥ 0.20 m or render as columns
Wall thickness `T = 0.10`. Any wall length < 0.20 m has a roughly square cross-section and renders as a column/pole. Either:
- Push doors flush against a corner (stub = 0)
- Keep stubs ≥ 0.20 m
- (Future) Add a check in the wall helper that collapses sub-threshold segments

### LDK base layer pattern
Defining LDK as the very first ROOMS entry covering the whole floor (`x=0..W, z=0..D`) and giving every other room a 1mm yOffset means LDK shows wherever no other room is painted. Saves having to define LDK as an irregular shape. Pattern is sound — keep it.

## Architecture and design notes

### Coordinate system (final, post X-flip)
- `z = 0` → BACK of building (LDK back wall, big sliding window in exterior)
- `z = D = 7.20` → FRONT (front door + porch)
- `x = 0` → building's LEFT (where washroom is in the 3D)
- `x = W = 6.30` → building's RIGHT (where genkan + stairs + corridor are in the 3D)
- `y = 0` → 1F floor level; `y = H = 2.40` → 1F ceiling

### The X-mirror convention (key insight from this session)
When mapping from blueprint to 3D code coordinates:
- Blueprint's page-LEFT → my code's RIGHT (`x = W`)
- Blueprint's page-RIGHT → my code's LEFT (`x = 0`)
- Blueprint's page-TOP → my code's `z = D` (front)
- Blueprint's page-BOTTOM → my code's `z = 0` (back)

So when reading the blueprint and converting to my code:
- Find the room's column on the page (col 1, 2, 3, or 4 from left)
- Map: blueprint col 1 (1.8 wide) → my x=4.5..6.3, blueprint col 2 (0.9) → my x=3.6..4.5, blueprint col 3 (1.8) → my x=1.8..3.6, blueprint col 4 (1.8) → my x=0..1.8
- Find the room's row on the page (rows from top)
- Map: blueprint top row → my code's `z` near `D`; blueprint bottom row → my code's `z` near 0.

### LDK base-layer trick
Define `ldk` as the first ROOMS entry covering the whole floor `x=0..W, z=0..D`. In `makeFloor`, every non-`ldk` room renders with `yOffset = 0.001` (1 mm above LDK). Result: LDK paints first, other rooms paint over it, leaving LDK visible only in the gaps. This means LDK takes whatever shape isn't claimed by other rooms — no need to compute its boundary explicitly.

### Wall helper conventions
- `wallX(group, z, a, b, gaps=[], height=H)` — runs along x axis at fixed z, with door gaps as `[xStart, xEnd]` arrays.
- `wallZ(group, x, a, b, gaps=[], height=H)` — runs along z axis at fixed x, with door gaps as `[zStart, zEnd]`.
- Both take a `gaps` array of `[start, end]` ranges. Wall splits into segments between gaps.
- Doors are full-height (floor to ceiling). Windows are NOT supported yet — they'd need a separate y-range parameter or a new helper.

### Why the corridor flows south into the LDK
The corridor in my final layout is `x=4.50..W, z=4.50..6.30`. Its south boundary at `z=4.50, x=4.50..5.40` has NO wall — corridor opens directly into the LDK. This is intentional: in Japanese houses, the corridor often opens into the LDK without a door, so I left a continuous floor with no wall. The stair door is at `x=5.40..W` on the same z=4.50 line, and the toilet door is on the corridor's west wall.
