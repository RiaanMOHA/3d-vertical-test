## Problems encountered and how they were solved

### Problem 1: PDF assumed to be 1F-only
- **Symptom**: Listed `ozu-test/blueprints/` and saw only `ozu-1-blueprint.pdf` (named like 1F-only). Initially thought there was no 2F blueprint.
- **Cause**: Filename suggests 1F-only but the PDF actually contains both floors side-by-side on a single page.
- **Fix**: Rendered PDF at 200 DPI and saw both panels. Re-rendered at 400 DPI to read 2F clearly.
- **Lesson**: Always render and LOOK before assuming a file's contents from its name. Same lesson as last session's "JPEG too small" excuse.

### Problem 2: Initial 2F crop produced a near-black image
- **Symptom**: Crop with `-resize` produced an image that displayed mostly black with two pink boxes barely visible.
- **Cause**: Aggressive resize introduced rendering artifacts in the cropped output.
- **Fix**: Re-render at higher base DPI (400 instead of 200), crop without resizing, use `+repage` to reset the page geometry after cropping.
- **Lesson**: Crop at native resolution. If you need a smaller image for context, crop a smaller region rather than resizing.

### Problem 3: Crop geometry warning
- **Symptom**: `magick … -crop 2200x900+0+1100 …` failed with "geometry does not contain image" and produced a 1x1 PNG.
- **Cause**: ImageMagick stores both the visible image dimensions and the original page geometry. The cropped 2F image was 2200x2700 visually, but its page geometry was still set to the parent PDF page (6617x4678) with offset (2350, 250). Subsequent `-crop` interpreted the offset against the page, not the visible image.
- **Fix**: Use `+repage` immediately after crops to reset the page geometry to match visible dimensions.

### Problem 4: Straight stair leads to a wall
- **Symptom**: User screenshot showed the stairs ending at the bedroom north wall with no exit.
- **Cause**: With a STRAIGHT stair, the 1F door (z=4.50) and the 2F exit can't both be on the corridor side. Top of the straight stair was at z=2.90 (back), exiting INTO bedroom_e north wall at z=2.90.
- **Fix**: Rebuilt as a **U-shape** (5+landing+6 risers). Both 1F bottom and 2F top are on the corridor side (z=4.50). Lower flight on west half, upper flight on east half, U-turn landing at the back.
- **Lesson**: For a stair to fit in a 0.9 × 1.6 footprint AND have entry/exit on the same side, it must turn. The blueprint's step labels (1-5 on 1F + 8-13 on 2F) explicitly indicate this — I should have built U-shape from the start.

### Problem 5: 2F storage was on the wrong side / blocking the path
- **Symptom**: Initial 2F layout placed storage at x=4.50..5.40 z=3.60..4.50, between the corridor and the stair shaft. The "corridor↔storage door" + "corridor↔stairs door" combination meant the user would have to walk through the storage closet to enter the stairs.
- **Cause**: I placed storage based on a quick blueprint read; on closer reading the blueprint may have storage at code-RIGHT (against the right wall, x=5.40..W) with stairs to its LEFT (x=4.50..5.40). My 1F has stairs at x=5.40..W (right edge), so for stairs to align between floors, the 2F stair must also be at x=5.40..W — which conflicts with the blueprint reading.
- **Fix (interim)**: Removed 2F storage entirely. Will re-add later in a position that doesn't break the corridor → stair flow.
- **Open question**: Is the 1F stair position correct (x=5.40..W) per the blueprint? May need to re-examine; if the blueprint shows the stair one column inward, both 1F and 2F would need adjustment. For now, the interpretation of "stair shaft = right edge" is preserved on both floors.

### Problem 6: Upper flight headroom is unrealistic
- **Symptom**: 2F landing slab at y=2.40 sits directly over the upper flight (x=5.85..W z=3.40..4.50). Headroom from upper flight tread tops to landing decreases from 1.09 m (step 6) to 0.22 m (step 10).
- **Cause**: 0.9 × 1.6 footprint is too small to hold a U-stair with code-compliant rise/run AND have viewing headroom under the landing.
- **Fix (deferred)**: Accept for MVP. Future: either carve a viewing hole in the landing above each step, or use semi-transparent landing material.

### PDF rendering with ImageMagick — always use `+repage`

After cropping, the PNG retains the parent page's geometry by default, which breaks subsequent crops. Always pass `+repage` to reset:

```
magick input.png -crop WxH+X+Y +repage output.png
```

### Helper: `step()` in stairs.js

The new `step(group, x, w, y, zNear, zFar, treadDepth)` helper builds a single tread + riser pair. The riser is placed at the larger of zNear/zFar (`Math.max`) so it faces the corridor side for BOTH the lower flight (rises on the high-z side) and the upper flight (rises on the high-z side after the U-turn).

## Architecture and design notes

### 2F coordinate system (X-mirror, post mirror)

```
z = 0   → BACK of building (room1, bedroom, bedroom_e back wall)
z = D   → FRONT of building (room2, room3, toilet front wall)
x = 0   → LEFT  of building (room3 / closets / room1)
x = W   → RIGHT of building (room2 / stair / bedroom_e)
y = 2.40 → 2F floor level (= 1F ceiling)
y = 4.80 → 2F ceiling
```

### 2F base-layer convention (different from 1F)

Unlike 1F where `ldk` is a base layer covering the whole floor and rooms paint over with `yOffset = 0.001`, the 2F has NO base layer. Each ROOMS entry is an independent rectangle, and `yOffset = 0` for all of them. The mid corridor + landing combine to fill the mid band; bedroom + bedroom_e + closets + room1 fill the bottom row; rooms 2/3 + toilet + corridor_n fill the top row. There's an explicit hole at x=5.40..W z=2.90..3.60 (no slab) for the stair shaft.

### Stair shaft geometry (U-shape)

```
Shaft footprint: x = 5.40..W (0.90 wide), z = 2.90..4.50 (1.60 deep)

Lower flight (1F → landing):
  x = 5.40..5.85 (west half, 0.45 wide)
  z = 4.50 (step 1, low) → 3.40 (step 5, high y=1.09)
  5 risers × 0.218 m, 5 treads × 0.220 m run

Landing platform (rear of shaft):
  x = 5.40..W (full width)
  z = 2.90..3.40 (depth 0.50)
  y = 1.09 (= 5 × RISE)

Upper flight (landing → 2F):
  x = 5.85..W (east half, 0.45 wide)
  z = 3.40 (step 6, low y=1.31) → 4.50 (step 11, top y=2.40)
  6 risers × 0.218 m, 6 treads × 0.183 m run
```
